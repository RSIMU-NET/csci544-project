( 2 ) roadways where insufficient space is allotted for vehicles to stop between a road intersection and nearby railroad tracks ; ( 3 ) junctions where railroad tracks are elevated above the surface of the roadway , exposing vehicles to the risk of getting hung on the tracks ; ( 4 ) light rail transit crossings without standards for their design , warning devices , or traffic control measures ; and ( 5 ) intersections where slowly moving vehicles , such as farm equipment , frequently cross the tracks . under the federal railroad safety act of 1970 , as amended , fra is responsible for regulating all aspects of railroad safety . fra's safety mission includes 1 ) establishing federal rail safety rules and standards ;
